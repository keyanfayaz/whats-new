# Format

## Length budget

- Daily: 150–250 words, max 8 bullets total across both sections.
- Weekly: 400–600 words, max 12 bullets across all sections.
- Monthly: 600–900 words, narrative paragraphs grouped by theme.

## Tone

Plainspoken, no hype. State what changed and why it matters in one line. Link
the source inline. Assume a smart reader who is short on time.

## Daily template

```
# What's new — YYYY-MM-DD

## AI

- <Headline> — <one line on what changed and why it matters>. [source](URL)

## Sports

- <Headline> — <one line on what changed and why it matters>. [source](URL)

_If a section has nothing genuinely new: "Nothing notable today."_
```

## Weekly template

```
# Weekly digest — YYYY-Www (week of YYYY-MM-DD)

## AI — the week in review

- <Theme or headline> — <2–3 sentence synthesis across the week>. [source](URL)

## Sports — the week in review

- <Theme or headline> — <2–3 sentence synthesis across the week>. [source](URL)

## Worth a second look

- <Item that still matters going into next week>. [source](URL)
```

## Monthly template

```
# Monthly archive — YYYY-MM

## AI

<2–4 narrative paragraphs grouped by theme: models, tools, industry/business.>

## Sports

<2–4 narrative paragraphs grouped by season/league storyline.>

## Threads to watch next month

- <Open storyline carrying forward>.
```
